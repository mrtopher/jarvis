# Company Research – Firstup

## Snapshot
- **What they do:** Intelligent employee/workforce communication platform — personalized comms to frontline and desk workers across an enterprise.
- **Size / stage:** Series E, ~$100M ARR (per company blog). Lean teams post-merger.
- **Funding / ownership:** ~$127M raised over 9 rounds, 23 investors.
- **HQ / locations:** San Francisco, CA. Founded 2008. Formed via merger of SocialChorus + Dynamic Signal.
- **Remote policy:** This role is Remote – US.

## Products & business model
> Enterprise SaaS. "Intelligent Communication Platform" — content delivery, targeting, analytics for internal comms. Serves 40 of the Fortune 100; reaches 17M+ employees daily. Forrester (June 2026) study cites 398% 3-year ROI, payback <6 months — actively used in sales motion.

## Tech stack
> AWS, Kubernetes, Ruby on Rails, Node, Python, PostgreSQL, Redshift. Agentic AI built on OpenAI SDK + MCP. Enterprise integrations: HRIS, ITSM, SharePoint, Teams.

## Culture & values
> Public framing: cares about people + work quality, rewards ideas regardless of source, welcomes ambiguity, values direct communication. (See red flags for the employee-reported counterpoint.)

## Reputation
> Glassdoor ~3.3/5 (271 reviews) but soft sub-scores: comp/benefits 2.7 (down 26% YoY), work-life 2.5, culture/values 2.4, career opportunities 2.2; only 24% would recommend. Recurring themes: post-merger layoffs, lean teams, leadership turnover, "culture of fear."

## Recent news
- Dec 2025: **Vinod Malhotra appointed CTO** — leads Engineering, AI/ML, CloudOps, Support. New leadership bench being built (this VP role is part of that build-out).
- June 2026: Forrester ROI study released (marketing milestone).
- Company announced reaching $100M ARR.

## Green flags
- Real scale and marquee logos (40 Fortune 100, 17M daily reach) — not a pre-PMF gamble.
- Heavy, genuine investment in agentic AI / platform — matches a senior AI-technical leader exactly.
- Brand-new CTO (Dec 2025) → org reset; a VP hire now has real mandate and influence.
- Strong comp band ($250–300K + bonus + equity) and fully remote.

## Red flags
- Repeated layoffs and lean teams post-merger; employees report instability and fear.
- Low morale and trust scores; "string of disappointing C-suite leaders" cited in reviews.
- Comp satisfaction falling — possible budget pressure despite the posted band.
- Series E + merger history can signal pressure for an exit / efficiency over growth.
