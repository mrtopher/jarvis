# Company Research – Change.org

## Snapshot
- **What they do:** World's largest petition and civic-action platform. Now building Change.vote, an election information product that helps voters explore their ballots.
- **Size / stage:** ~337 employees across 6 continents. 583M users as of May 2026. Mature platform, but the Elections team is an early-stage bet inside it.
- **Funding / ownership:** 100% nonprofit-owned. The Change.org Foundation oversees the Change.org Public Benefit Corporation (the tech, innovation, and growth arm). Historically raised ~$30M (last priced round 2017). Now self-sustaining on user contributions.
- **HQ / locations:** Distributed. No central HQ.
- **Remote policy:** Remote-first. This role is tagged #LI-Remote, USA.

## Products & business model
> Core product is the petition platform, funded by users who chip in to promote campaigns or become monthly members (moved off ads in 2019). Change.vote is the new growth vector: a mobile-first ballot and civic-information product. A 2025 pilot reached thousands of voters with strong engagement and a measurable lift in voter confidence. The 2026 goal is to scale it to millions nationwide.

## Tech stack
> Not disclosed in the posting. The role emphasizes a master data system that reconciles external authorities with AI extraction plus human-in-the-loop review to produce a provenanced civic data corpus. Signals heavy use of AI/LLM pipelines, data engineering, and consumer mobile. A separate "Senior Data Engineer, Elections" posting confirms the data-platform buildout.

## Culture & values
> Mission-driven: healthier democracies, giving people greater voice, bridging political divides. Open platform serving the full range of viewpoints (they are actively hiring across the political spectrum, e.g. a "Director of Conservative Content" role). Strong stated commitment to work/life balance and generous leave.

## Reputation
> Glassdoor ~4.2/5, 77% would recommend. Work/life balance rated 4.6, culture/values 4.3, career opportunities a weaker 3.7. Recurring complaint: "constant thrash" in product and process, and "too much product input from the executive team." New hires report it can be hard to build connections outside the immediate team in a fully remote org. At least one severe outlier review alleging a toxic/"racist" experience. History of repeated layoffs over recent years.

## Recent news
- Confirmed the transition to 100% nonprofit ownership (Foundation over PBC).
- Investing heavily in AI to build civic tools and scale Change.vote for the 2026 cycle.
- Actively expanding the Elections team (VP Eng, Senior Data Engineer, and related roles).

## Green flags
- Mission and remote-first culture align well with a senior, AI-forward leader.
- VP-level scope reporting directly to the CTO. Real ownership of a 0-to-millions product.
- The role is explicitly "AI forward," which matches an AI-consulting background closely.
- Strong work/life balance and leave.

## Red flags
- Compensation not disclosed. Nonprofit-owned PBC may pay below top-of-market for VP Eng.
- "Constant thrash" and heavy exec product input could frustrate a VP who owns technical vision.
- Repeated layoff history signals instability. Elections is a new, bet-the-cycle initiative.
- Election-year timeline pressure: bursty, high-stakes traffic with a hard calendar.
